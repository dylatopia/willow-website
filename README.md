# Willow Books & Library

> Built with AI-assisted tools and customized by the author. A front-end prototype developed for a Digital Business course project.

A bookstore-and-library marketplace concept, blending buying and borrowing into one calm, cottage-style reading experience. Willow lets users browse a curated catalog across genres — classic literature, Japanese literature, fantasy, mystery, self-growth, and romance — and choose to either purchase a book or borrow it for a lower fee.

## Concept

Most book platforms are either a store or a library, not both. Willow explores a hybrid model: every title can be bought outright or borrowed short-term at a fraction of the price, aimed at readers who want flexibility — collectors who want to own books, and casual readers who just want to read one and move on.

## Key Features (Prototype)

- **Homepage** — catalog stats (titles, books available to borrow, genres, average rating), editor's picks, and most popular books
- **Catalog browsing by genre** — Classic Literature, Japanese Literature, Fantasy & Fiction, Romance, Philosophy & Adventure, Magical Realism
- **Buy or Borrow option** on every book, each with separate pricing shown upfront
- **How it works** section — browse, buy or borrow, checkout, delivery in 2–4 business days
- **FAQ section** covering buy vs. borrow, loan periods, returns, payment methods, and shipping

## Tech Stack

Built using AI-assisted development tools (prompt-based / "vibe coding") for the initial structure, then customized manually — content, layout, and genre data — to match the intended concept and brand feel. Front-end only: HTML, CSS, JavaScript. This is a UI/UX prototype demonstrating the intended browsing and purchase/borrow flow — it is not yet connected to a backend, real inventory, or payment processing.

## Status

🚧 **Prototype stage.** Homepage, catalog browsing, and book detail flow are functional. Buy/borrow selection and checkout are simulated. Not yet connected to a live backend, payment gateway, or real book inventory.
